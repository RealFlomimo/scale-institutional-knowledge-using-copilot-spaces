# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **With Product Managers:** Collaborate on acceptance criteria and feature specifications
- **With Project Managers:** Participate in planning meetings and report blockers
- **With UX Designers:** Implement UI designs and provide technical feasibility feedback
- **With Technical Writers:** Contribute technical details for documentation
- **With Release Managers:** Provide deployment readiness status and support deployments
- **With QA/Testing:** Work with QA teams to validate acceptance criteria

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **With Project Managers:** Weekly syncs to align on priorities and dependencies
- **With Developers:** Define acceptance criteria and provide technical trade-off guidance
- **With UX Designers:** Partner on user research, wireframes, and usability validation
- **With Customer Success Managers:** Collect customer feedback and validate adoption metrics
- **With Technical Writers:** Ensure documentation reflects feature intent and usage

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **With Product Managers:** Weekly syncs to align on priorities and timeline impacts
- **With Developers:** Facilitate planning and track progress; escalate blockers
- **With Project Stakeholders:** Provide status updates and manage expectations
- **With Release Managers:** Coordinate deployment timelines and stakeholder communications
- **With QA/Testing:** Plan QA cycles and manage release readiness

---

## UX Designers

### Role Summary
UX Designers focus on understanding user needs, designing intuitive interfaces, and validating usability. They bridge user research and technical implementation to ensure features are both useful and easy to use.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and synthesize findings into design insights
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and design specifications
- Validate designs through user testing and iteration
- Collaborate with developers to ensure design implementation quality
- Document design decisions and maintain design systems

### Goals
- Deliver user-centered solutions that drive adoption
- Reduce friction in user workflows
- Ensure consistent, accessible design across products

### Typical Communication
- Design critiques and feedback sessions
- User research findings and recommendations
- Design specs and implementation guidelines
- Usability testing reports and iteration cycles

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **With Product Managers:** Partner on customer discovery, validate design decisions with user feedback
- **With Developers:** Provide design specs and iterate on implementation; address technical feasibility concerns
- **With Project Managers:** Provide design timeline estimates and flag scope changes
- **With Technical Writers:** Collaborate on in-product help content and user guides
- **With Customer Success Managers:** Gather user feedback post-launch to inform design iterations

---

## Technical Writers

### Role Summary
Technical Writers create clear, accurate documentation for internal processes and end-user audiences. They ensure knowledge is captured, organized, and accessible to support product adoption and team efficiency.

### Responsibilities
- Author and maintain internal process documentation and runbooks
- Create user guides, API documentation, and help content
- Collaborate with technical teams to capture accurate specifications
- Ensure consistency of tone, structure, and terminology
- Update documentation during release cycles and for major changes

### Goals
- Reduce support burden by providing clear, self-service documentation
- Accelerate team onboarding through comprehensive process docs
- Improve product adoption through effective user guides

### Typical Communication
- Documentation reviews with subject-matter experts
- Content planning and release notes preparation
- Feedback from support and customer success teams

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **With Developers:** Extract technical details, implementation notes, and API specifications
- **With Product Managers:** Understand feature intent and success criteria for context-appropriate documentation
- **With UX Designers:** Collaborate on in-product help, tooltips, and user-facing content
- **With Project Managers:** Coordinate documentation timing with release schedules
- **With Release Managers:** Prepare release notes and migration documentation
- **With Customer Success Managers:** Gather feedback on documentation gaps and user confusion points

---

## Release Managers

### Role Summary
Release Managers orchestrate the transition of completed work from development to production. They ensure deployments are safe, predictable, and well-communicated, minimizing risk and downtime.

### Responsibilities
- Plan and schedule release windows and deployment activities
- Verify pre-release checklists (testing, approvals, rollback plans)
- Coordinate with infrastructure and DevOps for deployment execution
- Document and communicate release details to stakeholders
- Execute rollback procedures if issues arise post-deployment
- Track and report deployment success metrics

### Goals
- Achieve zero unplanned outages through careful planning and validation
- Enable rapid, confident feature releases
- Maintain clear communication and transparency during deployments

### Typical Communication
- Release schedules and deployment plans
- Pre-release readiness reports
- Release notes and stakeholder announcements
- Post-deployment status and incident communications

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **With Project Managers:** Align on release timelines and coordinate stakeholder communications
- **With Developers:** Verify deployment readiness, gather last-minute fixes, support deployment execution
- **With QA/Testing:** Ensure all acceptance criteria are met before release authorization
- **With Technical Writers:** Ensure release notes are complete and accurate
- **With Customer Success Managers:** Provide post-release status and support onboarding communications

---

## Customer Success Managers

### Role Summary
Customer Success Managers maintain ongoing relationships with customers and internal stakeholders post-release. They gather feedback, ensure feature adoption, and create feedback loops that inform future product improvements.

### Responsibilities
- Collect and synthesize customer feedback on released features
- Monitor adoption metrics and identify adoption blockers
- Coordinate customer onboarding and enablement activities
- Report customer satisfaction and impact metrics to Product and Project Managers
- Escalate critical customer issues or feature requests
- Identify upsell and expansion opportunities based on usage patterns

### Goals
- Maximize customer adoption and satisfaction
- Create a feedback loop to inform prioritization and improvements
- Demonstrate ROI and business impact of delivered features

### Typical Communication
- Weekly/monthly customer feedback summaries
- Adoption and usage metric reports
- Customer success case studies and testimonials
- Priority escalations on critical issues or feature requests

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **With Product Managers:** Share customer feedback, adoption insights, and feature requests to inform prioritization
- **With UX Designers:** Report usability issues, user confusion points, and design improvement requests
- **With Project Managers:** Escalate critical customer issues and provide business context for prioritization
- **With Technical Writers:** Report documentation gaps or user confusion areas
- **With Release Managers:** Provide customer communication support and post-release adoption guidance
